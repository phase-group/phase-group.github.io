About the Group

The PHASE Group studies nonlinear and Hamiltonian dynamics in astrophysical gravitational systems, with particular emphasis on phase-space structure, orbital stability, and the emergence of chaos.

Our research spans galactic dynamics, compact objects, and relativistic systems, combining analytical theory, numerical simulations, and modern chaos diagnostics to understand long-term dynamical evolution.

Core Research Themes

Nonlinear Dynamics & Chaos

Investigation of regular and chaotic motion using phase-space analysis, Poincar√© sections, and chaos indicators such as SALI, mLCE, and basin entropy.

Phase-Space Structure

Study of invariant manifolds, resonances, and stability regions governing orbital motion in Hamiltonian gravitational systems.

Galactic Dynamics

Orbital dynamics in galactic cores and halo potentials, including the role of asymmetries, multipoles, and central compact objects.

Compact & Relativistic Objects

Dynamics around black holes and compact objects, incorporating spin effects, relativistic corrections, and strong-field behavior.

Hamiltonian & Lagrangian Formulations

Development and analysis of Hamiltonian and Lagrangian models to capture the fundamental structure of gravitational dynamics.

Numerical & Computational Methods

High-resolution numerical experiments, phase-space mapping, and systematic parameter-space exploration of dynamical regimes.

Our Approach

We focus on physically motivated models and rigorous dynamical-systems analysis, aiming to identify the fundamental mechanisms that generate complexity, instability, and order in astrophysical gravitational systems.
