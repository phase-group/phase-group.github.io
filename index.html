Introduction

The PHASE Group explores how order, chaos, and variability emerge in astrophysical systems governed by gravity.

Our research lies at the intersection of nonlinear dynamics, Hamiltonian mechanics, and astrophysics, with a central focus on phase-space structure, orbital stability, and chaotic transport in realistic gravitational environments.

We investigate particle and orbital dynamics in galactic cores, halo potentials, and strong-field regions around compact objects, where resonances, relativistic effects, and multipolar structure drive complex dynamical behavior.

A major thrust of our work connects theory with observations through X-ray timing analysis and QPO modeling, linking observed variability to nonlinear resonances and relativistic orbital motion in accretion flows and compact systems.

Current Research Topics

  • Nonlinear dynamics and chaos
  • Phase-space structure and stability
  • Galactic core and halo dynamics
  • Compact objects and relativistic motion
  • X-ray variability and QPO modeling
  • Chaos indicators (SALI, mLCE, basin entropy, etc.)
