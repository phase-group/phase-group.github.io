About the Group

The PHASE Group investigates nonlinear and Hamiltonian dynamics in astrophysical gravitational systems, with particular emphasis on phase-space structure, orbital stability, chaos, and time variability.

Our research spans galactic dynamics, compact objects, and relativistic systems, combining analytical theory, numerical simulations, and observational data analysis to understand the long-term evolution and variability of astrophysical systems.

Core Research Themes

Nonlinear Dynamics & Chaos

Regular and chaotic motion in gravitational systems studied through phase-space analysis, Poincar√© sections, Lyapunov exponents, SALI, and basin entropy.

Phase-Space Structure & Stability

Invariant manifolds, resonances, and stability regions governing orbital motion in Hamiltonian and Lagrangian gravitational models.

Galactic Dynamics

Orbital dynamics in galactic cores and halo potentials, including asymmetries, multipole structures, and the influence of central compact objects.

Compact & Relativistic Objects

Dynamics around black holes and compact objects, incorporating spin effects, relativistic corrections, and strong-field behavior.

X-ray Data Analysis & QPOs

Analysis of X-ray timing and spectral data from compact objects, focusing on quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs), variability patterns, and accretion-driven phenomena.

QPO Modeling & Theory

Physical and dynamical modeling of QPOs using nonlinear dynamics, orbital resonances, and relativistic motion in accretion and strong-gravity environments.

Our Approach

We adopt physically motivated models, rigorous dynamical-systems analysis, and reproducible numerical and data-driven methods to connect theoretical dynamics with observed variability in astrophysical systems.
